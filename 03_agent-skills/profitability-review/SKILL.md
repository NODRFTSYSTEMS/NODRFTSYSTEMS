# Profitability Review

## Use When

- pricing is being designed or revised
- the team wants to reduce wasted effort
- a workflow feels heavy relative to the revenue it supports

## Required Inputs

- service ladder or pricing model
- workflow or operating structure
- team model
- tool stack and recurring cost assumptions

## Workflow

1. Identify where labor is manual, duplicated, or founder-dependent.
2. Check whether pricing matches delivery complexity.
3. Flag tool overlap and hidden operational cost.
4. Distinguish:
   - critical margin risk
   - moderate inefficiency
   - optional optimization
5. Recommend changes that improve leverage without enterprise-heavy complexity.

## Outputs

- profitability findings
- margin risks
- recommended simplifications
- tool rationalization notes

## Do Not Do

- do not recommend more tools just because they exist
- do not assume premium pricing fixes weak execution
- do not optimize for theoretical scale at the expense of present margin
